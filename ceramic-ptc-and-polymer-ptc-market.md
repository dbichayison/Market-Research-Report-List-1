<p><p>Ceramic PTC (Positive Temperature Coefficient) and Polymer PTC are two types of temperature-sensitive resistors used for overcurrent protection in electronic devices. PPTC (Polymeric PTC) devices utilize polymer-based materials that exhibit increased resistance at elevated temperatures, thereby limiting current flow. CPTC (Ceramic PTC), on the other hand, uses ceramic materials that operate similarly but may offer different thermal and electrical characteristics. Both types are critical in ensuring device reliability and safety in various applications, including automotive, consumer electronics, and industrial equipment.</p></p>
