<p><p>The glass fiber market is divided into two primary types: general-purpose glass fibers and special-purpose glass fibers. General-purpose glass fibers are commonly used in a wide range of applications, including construction, automotive, and consumer goods, due to their excellent strength, lightweight, and cost-effectiveness. Special-purpose glass fibers, on the other hand, are engineered for specific applications requiring unique properties, such as high-temperature resistance, chemical durability, or electrical insulation, making them ideal for specialized industries like aerospace, electronics, and marine.</p></p>
