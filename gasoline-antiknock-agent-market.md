<p><p>The gasoline antiknock agent market is primarily categorized into two types: ethers and metal antiknock agents. Ethers, such as MTBE (methyl tert-butyl ether), enhance fuel octane ratings while reducing emissions. Metal antiknock agents, like tetraethyl lead, were traditionally used but have largely been phased out due to environmental concerns. Ethers are favored in modern formulations for their cleaner combustion properties, while metal agents are now rarely used due to regulatory restrictions and toxicity issues.</p></p>
