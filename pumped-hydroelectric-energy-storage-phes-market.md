<p><p>Pumped Hydroelectric Energy Storage (PHES) is a technology that stores and generates electricity by using two water reservoirs at different elevations. During periods of low electricity demand, excess energy is used to pump water from the lower reservoir to the upper reservoir. When demand peaks, the stored water is released back down through turbines to generate electricity. This method provides a reliable and efficient means of energy storage, particularly for integrating renewable energy sources like wind and solar power.</p><p>The PHES market is experiencing significant growth driven by the increasing need for energy storage solutions that can manage the intermittency of renewable energy sources. Factors such as government incentives for clean energy, the need for grid stability, and environmental concerns are fueling investments in PHES facilities. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.6% during the forecast period. Additionally, advancements in technology and expanding applications, including large-scale energy storage and ancillary services, are enhancing the market's potential. As the global focus shifts towards sustainable energy solutions, PHES is poised to play a crucial role in the energy transition.</p></p>
